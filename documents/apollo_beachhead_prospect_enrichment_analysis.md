## Engagement Strategy by Decision-Maker Type

### Provost/Academic Leaders (Priority: HIGH)
**Best Engagement Angle**: Accreditation support, institutional effectiveness measurement, academic reputation building

**Key Pain Points**:
- Complex accreditation reporting across multiple agencies
- Alumni outcome tracking for regional/specialized accreditation bodies
- Limited visibility into graduate career trajectories
- Need for standardized impact measurement framework

**Messaging Focus**: 
- Reduce accreditation compliance burden
- Quantify institutional impact on student/alumni outcomes
- Support strategic planning with alumni data intelligence

### VPs of Institutional Research (Priority: CRITICAL)
**Best Engagement Angle**: Direct product buyers, data infrastructure, reporting automation

**Key Pain Points**:
- Manual data aggregation from multiple sources (LinkedIn, alumni surveys, public records)
- Time-intensive IPEDS reporting
- Limited alumni employment outcome visibility
- Need for scalable data pipeline

**Messaging Focus**:
- Automate alumni outcomes research at scale
- Reduce research team workload
- Enable self-service institutional effectiveness reporting
- Support evidence-based strategic planning

### VPs of Advancement (Priority: HIGH)
**Best Engagement Angle**: Fundraising intelligence, donor cultivation, alumni engagement

**Key Pain Points**:
- Limited visibility into alumni career success and earning potential
- Difficulty identifying high-potential donors by career trajectory
- Manual prospect research consuming staff time
- Need for data-driven cultivation strategies

**Messaging Focus**:
- Identify highest-value donor prospects by career success metrics
- Demonstrate institutional impact to enhance donor engagement
- Automate alumni intelligence gathering
- Support evidence-based fundraising strategy
