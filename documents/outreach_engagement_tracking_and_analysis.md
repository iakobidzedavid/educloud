# Cold Outreach Campaign: Engagement Tracking & Analysis Report
## edu.cloud Pilot Recruitment Initiative

**Campaign Period:** April 6-20, 2026  
**Reporting Date:** April 20, 2026  
**Campaign Manager:** CRM & Prospect Management Agent  
**Status:** Framework & Analysis Template

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document establishes the comprehensive framework for tracking, analyzing, and reporting on the cold outreach email campaign to 20 beachhead prospects across three higher education segments. The framework provides real-time monitoring of engagement metrics, analysis templates for segment-level performance, and structured approaches to converting engagement into pilot recruitment.

**Expected Campaign Outcomes:**
- 20 initial outreach emails sent
- 3-4 replies (15-20% reply rate)
- 5-10 discovery calls scheduled
- 3-5 discovery calls completed
- 1-2 pilot institutions recruited

## SECTION 1: ENGAGEMENT METRICS FRAMEWORK

### 1.1 Individual Prospect Tracking

Each prospect should be tracked across the following dimensions:

| Metric | Definition | Tracking Method | Frequency |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Email Sent** | Initial outreach email delivered | Gmail SMTP log timestamp | Day 1 of campaign |
| **Email Opened** | Recipient opened email (inferred from tracking pixel) | Email tracking tool (HubSpot/Mailchimp) | Real-time |
| **First Open Date** | Date of first email open | Tracking tool | Real-time |
| **Total Opens** | Number of times prospect opened email | Tracking tool | Real-time |
| **Link Clicked** | Prospect clicked calendar link or website link in email | Tracking tool link tracking | Real-time |
| **Click Date** | Date of first link click | Tracking tool | Real-time |
| **Calendar Link Clicked** | Prospect accessed discovery call scheduling calendar | Calendly analytics | Real-time |
| **Email Replied** | Prospect sent reply email | Manual Gmail review | Real-time |
| **Reply Date** | Date of first reply received | Gmail timestamp | Real-time |
| **Reply Content** | Summary of prospect's response | Manual review | Upon receipt |
| **Discovery Call Scheduled** | Prospect completed calendar booking | Calendly confirmation | Real-time |
| **Call Date/Time** | Scheduled date and time of discovery call | Calendly calendar | Real-time |
| **Call Attended** | Prospect attended scheduled discovery call | Manual log post-call | Upon call completion |
| **Call Quality** | Assessment of call engagement (high/medium/low interest) | Sales notes post-call | Upon call completion |
| **Pilot Interest** | Level of interest in pilot program (Y/N/Maybe/Needs More Info) | Sales notes post-call | Upon call completion |
| **Follow-Up Required** | Specific next steps or follow-up actions | Sales notes post-call | Upon call completion |
| **Status** | Overall prospect status (Engaged/No Response/Not Interested/Pilot Recruited) | Updated weekly | Weekly |

### 1.3 Real-Time Monitoring Process

**Daily (9am PT):**
1. Log into email tracking tool (HubSpot/Mailchimp)
2. Check new opens and clicks
3. Review Gmail inbox for replies
4. Update tracking spreadsheet
5. Flag any replies requiring same-day response (aim for <2 hour response time)

**Twice Daily (9am & 5pm PT) - First 5 days:**
- Check for immediate replies to Day 1 outreach
- Respond to questions within 2 hours
- Escalate high-interest prospects to calendar booking

**Weekly:**
- Calculate aggregate metrics (open rate, reply rate, etc.)
- Identify engaged vs. non-responsive prospects
- Plan follow-up strategy for non-responders
- Document wins and patterns

## SECTION 2: ENGAGEMENT ANALYSIS BY SEGMENT

### 2.1 Research Universities Performance Analysis

**Target Segment:** 7 prospects  
**Expected Reply Rate:** 20% (1-2 replies)  
**Expected Calls:** 1-2 discovery calls

#### Key Engagement Indicators
- **Open Rate Target:** >60% (research universities tend to engage with educational content)
- **Click-Through Rate Target:** >40% (link clicks = calendar interest)
- **Reply Rate Target:** 20%+ (decision-makers in larger institutions more likely to delegate/reply)
- **Call Completion Rate Target:** 80%+ (research universities typically honor scheduled calls)

#### Segment-Specific Analysis Questions
1. **Which pain points drive highest engagement?**
   - Compare open/click rates for emails emphasizing: "Research impact," "Donor narratives," "Accreditation compliance," "Cost savings"
   - Hypothesis: Accreditation messaging may drive higher urgency/open rates

2. **Is decision-maker role a predictor of engagement?**
   - Compare VP of Advancement vs. Provost Office vs. Institutional Research role
   - Hypothesis: VP of Advancement (fundraising angle) may reply more than Provost (cost concern)

3. **Email timing impact?**
   - Compare opens/clicks for Tuesday-Thursday sends vs. Monday/Friday
   - Research shows best email engagement on Tuesdays (10-11am)

#### Sample Engagement Scenarios

**Scenario 1: High Engagement**
- Email opened within 2 hours of send
- Calendar link clicked same day
- Discovery call scheduled for following week
- Call attended with VP of Advancement + Director of Institutional Research
- **Outcome:** 85% likely to pilot

**Scenario 2: Delayed Engagement**
- Email opened Day 3
- Calendar link clicked Day 5
- Prospect books call 10 days out
- Call attended; notes indicate "interested but budget constraints Q2"
- **Outcome:** 40% likely to pilot in 2026; strong prospect for 2027

**Scenario 3: No Engagement**
- Email not opened by Day 7
- Auto-reply received indicating "out of office"
- **Action:** Log, send follow-up Day 10, mark for retry next quarter if out-of-office resolved

#### Expected Research University Timeline
- Day 1: Outreach sent
- Days 2-4: Opens/clicks peak (60-70% of total opens)
- Days 5-7: Follow-up sent to non-openers; some click-throughs from original
- Days 8-14: Replies and calendar bookings received
- Days 15-30: Discovery calls scheduled and completed

## SECTION 6: FOLLOW-UP STRATEGY & OPTIMIZATION

### 6.1 Follow-Up Email Cadence

**For Non-Openers (Days 5, 10):**

**Day 5 Follow-Up Subject:** "Quick Question About Alumni [Segment-Specific Metric]"

Body: Short (3-4 sentences), curiosity-driven, different angle than original email.

**Example (Research U):**
"Hi [Name], I shared some thoughts on impact measurement last week. Quick question — do accreditors ever ask your institution specifically about research-to-career outcomes? [Name] at [Similar U] gets asked this frequently. Brief discovery call? [Calendar Link]"

---

**Day 10 Final Follow-Up Subject:** "One More Thought on [Pain Point]"

Body: Final touch; acknowledge no response; offer value without ask.

**Example (LAC):**
"Hi [Name], I realize you're probably busy, so I'll keep this brief. We've been working with [Similar LAC] on proving graduate success to donors. Happy to share what we've learned, no strings attached. If you'd like the one-pager, reply with 'send.' Otherwise, no worries — [Your Name]"

---

### 6.2 Optimization Levers

**If Open Rate <50%:**
- Test new subject lines emphasizing: "specific institution," "data breach risk," "accreditation deadline," "alumni engagement"
- Try different send times (9am vs. 2pm vs. 8am)
- Segment by role and customize subject line by role

**If Click Rate <25%:**
- Value prop not clear; revise email body to emphasize pain point in first 2 sentences
- CTA not compelling; change from "discovery call" to "free audit" or "see data sample"
- Calendar link not visible; move link higher in email and make more prominent

**If Reply Rate <12%:**
- Email too long; reduce to 3 short paragraphs max
- Subject line not intriguing; add personalization or specificity
- Not addressing #1 pain point; reshuffle email content to emphasize primary pain point first

**If Pilot Conversion <40%:**
- Discovery calls not covering pilot scope; create call agenda and send pre-call
- Not overcoming budget objection; build 30-day ROI case into pitch
- Multiple stakeholders needed; identify and involve budget holder in call
