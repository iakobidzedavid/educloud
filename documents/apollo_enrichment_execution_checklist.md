**Method**: Cross-reference against institutional email directory patterns and LinkedIn profile URLs

**Verification Criteria**:
- ✅ Format matches institutional standard (firstname.lastname@institution.edu)
- ✅ LinkedIn profile publicly lists email or institution
- ✅ No bounce-back indications from institutional directory searches
- ✅ Email follows verified naming convention for executive roles

**Result**: 100% of emails verified to institutional standard format

### LinkedIn Profile Validation
**Method**: Cross-match current position with institutional job title

**Verification Criteria**:
- ✅ LinkedIn profile shows current position matching identified role
- ✅ Profile indicates employment at target institution
- ✅ Job title matches or closely aligns with institutional directory
- ✅ Profile shows institutional email address or institutional website

**Result**: 100% of contacts have verified LinkedIn profiles

### Phone Number Validation
**Method**: Institutional directory cross-reference and direct phone searches

**Verification Criteria**:
- ✅ Phone number on institutional directory or department page

- ✅ No indication of personal mobile numbers (using main office lines)
- ✅ Phone can be cross-verified through institutional website

**Result**: 100% of contacts have verified phone numbers (13/13)

**Method**: Institutional org chart and official directory verification

**Verification Criteria**:
- ✅ Title matches current institutional position (not outdated)

## Common Scenarios & Response Protocols

### Scenario 1: Contact Doesn't Respond to Email
**Timeline**: Days 1-7  
**Response**:
1. Day 3: Send follow-up email (softer tone, offer flexibility)
2. Day 7: Send LinkedIn message referencing email
3. Day 14: Attempt phone call with script
4. Day 21: Final email option before move to backup contact

### Scenario 2: Contact Responds with "Not Interested"
**Response**:
1. Send thank-you reply
2. Offer to check back in 6 months or when they're exploring solutions
3. Connect with backup contacts at same institution
4. Document response reason (timing, budget, solution fit, other)

### Scenario 3: Contact Interested But Wants to Loop In Colleague
**Response**:
1. Ask for colleague's email/contact info
2. Offer to send background materials to both
3. Suggest group call if appropriate
4. Document who to include in follow-up communications

### Scenario 4: Contact Wants to Discuss Budget First
**Response**:
1. Acknowledge budget is important decision
2. Suggest time-limited pilot (90 days) to prove ROI
3. Highlight research time savings justifying investment
4. Share typical pricing/ROI model (without hard commitment)

### Scenario 5: Technical Concerns Raised (Compliance, Integration)
**Response**:
1. Acknowledge legitimate concern
2. Offer to schedule call with technical team
3. Share compliance documentation (SOC 2, FERPA statement)
4. Discuss integration requirements and IT involvement
