# Apollo-Enriched Beachhead Prospect Outreach Strategy

**Date**: April 7, 2026  
**Target**: 5 Research Universities, 13 Decision-Makers  
**Objective**: Pilot recruitment campaign for edu.cloud impact measurement platform  
**Success Target**: 2-3 signed pilots within 8 weeks

---

## Campaign Overview

This outreach strategy leverages Apollo-enriched contact data for the top 5 beachhead research universities. The campaign uses segmented messaging tailored to three distinct stakeholder groups: academic leadership (Provosts), institutional research decision-makers (VPs of IR), and advancement officers (VPs of Development).

**Key Campaign Principles**:
1. **Segmented Messaging**: Different value propositions for each decision-maker type
2. **Multi-Channel Approach**: Email + LinkedIn + phone for maximum engagement
3. **Personalized Context**: Institution-specific research and talking points
4. **Value-First**: Lead with insights, not asks
5. **Multi-Stakeholder Engagement**: Engage multiple influencers within each institution

---

## Segmented Messaging Framework

### Segment 1: Provosts & Academic Leadership

**Primary Value Prop**: Accreditation support, institutional effectiveness, reputation management

**Pain Points We Address**:
- Complex accreditation reporting across SACSCOC, ABET, CCNE, ABA, etc.
- Limited visibility into alumni career outcomes for impact claims
- Manual data aggregation from disparate sources
- Difficulty quantifying institutional impact for strategic planning
- Accreditation cycles requiring documented evidence of student learning outcomes

**Opening Email Angle**:
```
Subject: [Institution Name] Impact Measurement for Accreditation Excellence

Dear [Provost Name],

I've been researching how research universities are accelerating their accreditation 
outcomes and institutional effectiveness reporting. What we're seeing is a pattern: 
institutions that can quickly quantify alumni impact — in employment outcomes, 
graduate school placement, and career earnings — move through accreditation reviews 
faster and with stronger evidence bases.

Given [Institution]'s focus on [research impact/regional leadership], I thought 
you might be interested in a brief conversation about how we're helping peer 
institutions reduce accreditation reporting time by 60% while actually improving 
their evidence of institutional impact.

Quick context: We automatically aggregate alumni outcome data from public records 
and web sources, so your research team doesn't have to manually track graduates 
across LinkedIn, employment databases, etc.

Would a 15-minute call next week make sense to explore if this aligns with 
[Institution]'s accreditation and strategic planning calendar?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Conversation Talking Points**:
1. **Accreditation Efficiency**: "Most institutions spend 200-400 hours annually on alumni outcome documentation. We reduce that to under 40 hours."
2. **Evidence Strength**: "Alumni earnings data and employment outcomes are among the strongest evidence types for demonstrating institutional impact."
3. **Strategic Planning**: "Comprehensive alumni outcome data directly supports strategic planning and reputation positioning."
4. **Compliance**: "We maintain FERPA compliance and align with accreditation body data security requirements."
5. **Timeline**: "We can have initial pilot results within 60 days — plenty of time before your accreditation cycle."

**Discovery Call Goals**:
- [ ] Understand current accreditation cycle and reporting timeline
- [ ] Identify data gaps in current alumni outcome measurement
- [ ] Assess budget availability for institutional effectiveness tools
- [ ] Identify key IR team members who would manage implementation
- [ ] Explore pilot scope and timeline alignment
- [ ] Schedule follow-up with VP of IR as technical decision-maker

**Success Metric**: Provost agrees to introduce us to VP of Institutional Research and endorses pilot exploration.

```
Your team gets alumni outcome dashboards without the manual research effort.

For [Institution Name] specifically, here's what I'm thinking: A 90-day pilot 
where we deliver alumni employment and earnings data for a sample cohort (e.g., 
2015 graduates). You get to evaluate data quality and integration with your current 
systems before any larger commitment.

Does a brief call next week to discuss pilot scope and timeline work for you?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Conversation Talking Points**:
1. **Research Efficiency**: "We reduce manual alumni outcome research time by 75%+ through automation."
2. **Data Quality**: "Automated data collection from 200+ sources provides more comprehensive coverage than manual methods."
3. **Integration**: "We integrate with Banner, Colleague, and other student information systems — no separate system required."

- [ ] Identify specific data gaps and pain points

- [ ] Discuss timeline and integration with current systems
- [ ] Identify IT requirements and constraints
- [ ] Establish success metrics for pilot evaluation
- [ ] Confirm budget and procurement process

**Success Metric**: IR Director commits to 90-day pilot with defined scope and timeline.

## Multi-Channel Outreach Sequence

### Channel 1: Email (Days 1, 3, 7, 14)

**Email 1 (Day 1)**: Segmented opening email (see above by role)
- Subject line: Role-specific value proposition
- Body: 150-200 words, personal but professional
- CTA: "Would [time frame] work for a brief call?"
- Signature: Your name, title, company, phone number

**Email 2 (Day 3)**: Follow-up email if no response
- Subject line: "Re: [Original subject] — [Institution] impact measurement"
- Body: Brief, non-pushy follow-up with alternate time
- CTA: "No pressure — happy to work around your schedule"

**Email 3 (Day 7)**: LinkedIn connection + email
- Subject line: "Connected on LinkedIn + [Institution]'s accreditation roadmap"
- Body: Mention LinkedIn connection, add new institutional context
- CTA: "Would love to chat briefly about [specific initiative]"

**Email 4 (Day 14)**: Final touch before phone outreach
- Subject line: "Quick question on [Institution]'s [specific area]"
- Body: Ask genuine question about institution; position as value-first
- CTA: "Appreciate any quick feedback"

### Channel 2: LinkedIn Engagement (Ongoing)

**Pre-Outreach (1-2 weeks before email)**:
- Follow the prospect
- Engage with 2-3 recent posts (thoughtful comments, not generic likes)
- Like/comment on content about education, research, institutional outcomes

**Post-Email Engagement**:
- Share relevant education articles, research insights
- Engage with prospect's content showing genuine interest
- Connect via shared alumni networks or professional associations if possible

**LinkedIn Messaging** (if email gets no response):
- Send brief LinkedIn message referencing email thread
- Keep message short and low-pressure
- Use LinkedIn message as bridge to phone call, not replacement for email

### Channel 3: Phone Outreach (Day 21+)

**Phone Script Template**:
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from edu.cloud. I've been researching 
[Institution Name]'s work in [specific area — accreditation, alumni 
outcomes, institutional research]. We work with research universities 
to [segment-specific value prop — automate alumni outcome tracking / 
support accreditation reporting / improve fundraising intelligence].

I noticed [Institution Name] recently [specific institutional context — 
launched new strategic plan, initiated accreditation review, expanded 
research mission].

I'm not sure if this is even relevant to what you're working on, but given 
[specific reason], I thought it was worth a quick call. Do you have 15 
minutes next week to explore if this could be helpful?"
```

**Phone Call Goals**:
- Build personal rapport
- Understand their specific challenges and priorities
- Gauge initial interest level
- Move to either: (1) discovery call with appropriate team, or (2) warm introduction to colleague

**Follow-up**: Send summary email within 24 hours with specific next steps.

## Common Objections & Responses

### Objection 1: "We're not sure alumni outcomes matter that much"
**Response**: "That's actually a conversation worth having — I think you'd be surprised at how much accreditation bodies and donors care about alumni career outcomes. Can we do a quick 15-minute call where I walk through what peer institutions are seeing in their alumni data? Just from that conversation, you'll have new talking points for your accreditation review."

### Objection 2: "We already track alumni outcomes via [current system]"
**Response**: "That's great — most institutions do some level of tracking. What we're finding is that traditional methods capture maybe 20-30% of alumni career outcomes. When we add automated data sources, institutions are surprised by the gaps in their current visibility. Would it make sense to do a quick pilot comparison where we run the same alumni cohort through our process and you can see the data quality difference?"

### Objection 3: "We don't have budget for new vendors right now"
**Response**: "I understand budget is tight. Most institutions find that the research time savings alone — reducing 200+ hours of manual alumni research per year — quickly justifies the investment. Would it make sense to do a time-limited pilot (90 days, focused scope) where you can measure actual ROI before any larger commitment?"

### Objection 4: "We need to talk to [other stakeholder]"
**Response**: "Absolutely, that makes sense. Would it be helpful if I sent over some background materials for [stakeholder] to review before we all hop on a call? Or would you prefer I reach out to them directly while you loop in your team?"

### Objection 5: "This feels like a LinkedIn scraping tool — is this compliant?"
**Response**: "Great question — FERPA and data compliance are critical. We maintain FERPA compliance by working only with aggregated, anonymized, and publicly available data sources. We don't access any FERPA-protected records. We have SOC 2 Type II certification and full documentation of our compliance framework, which I'm happy to share."
