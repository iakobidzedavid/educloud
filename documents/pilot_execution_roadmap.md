# Pilot Execution Roadmap
## Phase-Based Implementation Plan with Unit Economics Targets

**Document Date:** April 7, 2026  
**Duration:** 12 months (Months 1-12)  
**Key Objective:** Recruit 10 pilot customers, validate unit economics assumptions, achieve Year 1 profitability

---

## Executive Overview

This roadmap translates the business model unit economics into concrete, month-by-month execution steps. It sequences pilot recruitment, product delivery, and customer success activities to validate the assumptions in the LTV/CAC analysis while maintaining a clear path to profitability.

**Three Critical Success Factors:**
1. **Recruit 5+ customers by Month 6** (validates $45k ACV, $4.2k CAC assumptions)
2. **Achieve 90%+ retention at Month 12** (validates 90% Y1 retention assumption)
3. **Maintain >85% gross margin** (validates product cost structure)

If all three are achieved, proceed to Phase 2 scaling. If any is missed, pause growth and diagnose the gap.

## Phase 1: Pilot Recruitment (Months 1-6)

### Goal: Sign 5 pilot customers, establish product/market fit signals

#### Month 1: Campaign Preparation

**Primary Objective:** Build outreach infrastructure; identify and validate top 50 prospects

**Activities:**

1. **Prospect Identification** (Lead by: BDR/Sales)
   - Use Apollo data (existing from prior enrichment task) to segment institutions:
     - Research Universities (R1/R2): Top 30 by endowment/alumni count
     - Liberal Arts Colleges: Top 50 by selectivity + advancement spend
     - Community Colleges: Top 30 by alumni base size
   - Create target list of 50 with:
     - Institution name, size, alumni count
     - Advancement/IR/Institutional Research contact names + emails
     - Estimated budget tier (small/medium/large)
   - **Output:** Segmented prospect list in CRM (HubSpot or Airtable)

2. **Messaging Development** (Lead by: Founder/Product)
   - Create 3 email templates tailored by institution type:
     - **Research Universities:** Focus on accreditation reporting, rankings impact, alumni network ROI
     - **Liberal Arts:** Focus on advancement outcomes, donor reporting, student recruitment
     - **Community Colleges:** Focus on completion/employment tracking, workforce alignment
   - Develop 1-minute pitch explaining value prop + pilot program
   - Create case study template (for post-pilot use with real data)
   - **Output:** 3 email templates + 1-pager positioning

3. **Landing Page & Lead Capture** (Lead by: Marketer)
   - Build lightweight landing page explaining pilot program:
     - Value proposition (clearly articulated from market research)
     - Pilot benefits (free 6-month assessment, exec steering committee)
     - How to apply (CTA to discovery call)
   - Integrate with Calendly for scheduling
   - Set up email automations for follow-up sequences
   - **Output:** Live landing page + automation workflows

4. **Sales Collateral Creation** (Lead by: Founder/Product)
   - Build 10-slide pitch deck covering:
     - Problem (pain points from market research)
     - Solution (how edu.cloud solves it)
     - Methodology (data sources, accuracy, timeline)
     - Outcomes (example alumni profiles, impact data)
     - Pilot program terms (6 months, free trial, reference customer agreement)
   - Create ROI calculator template
   - Build competitive comparison one-pager
   - **Output:** Pitch deck + ROI calc + comp analysis

**Key Metrics (End of M1):**
- ✓ 50 prospects identified and loaded into CRM
- ✓ 3 email templates created and tested
- ✓ Landing page live
- ✓ Pitch deck finalized

**CAC Budget:** $0 spend (preparation only)

#### Month 2: Launch Outreach Campaign

**Primary Objective:** Execute cold outreach to 50 prospects; book 6-8 initial discovery calls

**Activities:**

1. **Email Campaign Launch** (Lead by: BDR)
   - Week 1: Send initial email to 20 research universities (highest ACV potential)
   - Week 2: Send to 15 liberal arts colleges
   - Week 3: Send to 15 community colleges
   - Week 4: Follow-up sequence to non-openers
   
   **Email Structure:**
   - Subject line: Personalized (institution name, specific pain point)
   - Body: 3-4 sentences mentioning recent finding relevant to their institution + value prop
   - CTA: 15-minute discovery call (link to Calendly)
   - Signature: Founder name + title
   
   **Tracking:**
   - Log all opens, clicks, bounces in CRM
   - Document non-responders for follow-up
   - Record response time to identify engaged prospects

2. **Initial Discovery Calls** (Lead by: BDR + Founder on key calls)
   - Target: 6-8 calls booked this month
   - Duration: 15 minutes
   - Agenda:
     1. Intro + understanding of their alumni tracking current state (3 min)
     2. Brief pitch on edu.cloud + methodology (5 min)
     3. Pilot program overview (3 min)
     4. Qualify budget + timeline (2 min)
     5. Next step: Technical demo or pass to advancement team (2 min)
   - Note decision criteria: Will they participate in 6-month free pilot?
   - **Output:** Discovery call notes + qualification status for each prospect

3. **Call Prep & Follow-Up** (Lead by: BDR)
   - Research each institution before call (alumni demographics, recent news, strategy)
   - Send follow-up email within 2 hours of call with:
     - Next steps clearly stated
     - Link to demo if qualified
     - Timeline to decision

4. **CAC Tracking** (Lead by: Finance/Operations)
   - Begin tracking time spent on outreach:
     - Prospect research hours
     - Email personalization hours
     - Call prep and execution hours
   - Document all expenses:
     - Email tool costs
     - Landing page hosting
     - Tools/subscriptions
   - Allocate proportionally to customers eventually acquired

**Key Metrics (End of M2):**
- ✓ 50 emails sent
- ✓ 20+ opens (40% open rate target)
- ✓ 8+ click-throughs (16% CTR target)
- ✓ 6-8 discovery calls booked
- ✓ 4+ prospects qualified (budget, timeline, fit)
- ✓ 0-1 pilot interest (early-stage)

**CAC Budget:** $4,200 (1 customer × $4.2k CAC) allocated to early activities

#### Month 3: Demo & Evaluation Phase

**Primary Objective:** Convert 2-3 discovery calls to pilot agreements

**Activities:**

1. **Technical Demos** (Lead by: Founder/Product)
   - Target: 4-6 demos scheduled (25-30 min each)
   - Demo content:
     - Live platform walkthrough (2 alumni profiles, outcome tracking, reporting)
     - Data methodology explanation (web scraping + public records methodology)
     - Customization possibilities (by institution type, fields, integrations)
     - Timeline & onboarding process (4-week implementation)
     - Support during pilot (monthly check-ins, technical support)
   - Capture feedback:
     - Most valuable features
     - Concerns/objections
     - Implementation requirements
     - Who needs to sign off internally (IR, Advancement, CFO)
   - Follow-up: Send demo recording + pilot proposal within 24 hours

2. **Pilot Program Agreements** (Lead by: Founder + Legal)
   - Prepare pilot terms document:
     - 6-month free trial
     - Free data assessment (first 500 alumni)
     - Monthly steering committee calls
     - NDA + data protection agreement
     - Reference customer commitment (1-2 calls post-pilot)
     - Path to commercial contract (if successful)
   - Customize per institution:
     - Segment data fields relevant to their alumni base
     - Reporting frequency/format preferences
   - Include legal review of privacy/data handling

3. **Internal Stakeholder Engagement** (Lead by: BDR as coordinator)
   - Identify buying committee at each prospect:
     - VP of Advancement
     - Institutional Research Director
     - CFO (budget approval)
     - CIO (data security approval)
   - Prepare stakeholder-specific value props:
     - Advancement: Donor engagement intelligence, campaign ROI
     - IR: Accreditation data, rankings support
     - CFO: Cost per outcome vs. consulting quotes
     - CIO: Data security architecture, compliance
   - Distribute stakeholder-facing materials before final signing

4. **Objection Handling** (Lead by: Founder)
   - Prepare responses to likely objections:
     - "How do we know your data is accurate?" → Methodology transparency, audit trail
     - "Is this a data privacy risk?" → FERPA compliance, encryption, no student PII
     - "What if we don't like the results?" → 6-month free trial = no financial risk
     - "Can we integrate with our ERP/CRM?" → Yes, via API; roadmap shared
   - Document all objections and responses for future reference

**Key Metrics (End of M3):**
- ✓ 4-6 demos executed
- ✓ 2-3 pilot proposals sent
- ✓ 1-2 pilot agreements signed (Target: 2)
- ✓ 10+ engaged prospects in evaluation phase
- ✓ Average time from first email to pilot signature: 30-45 days

**CAC Budget:** $8,400 (2 customers × $4.2k CAC)

#### Month 4: First Implementations

**Primary Objective:** Launch 2+ pilots; establish product/market fit patterns

**Activities:**

1. **Pilot Launch & Onboarding** (Lead by: Founder + Customer Success)
   - For each signed pilot:
     - Kick-off call with steering committee (establish success metrics + timeline)
     - Technical setup (data access, API authentication, reporting database)
     - Data ingestion phase 1 (first 500 alumni)
     - Dashboard configuration (align to their KPIs)
     - Training on platform navigation + reporting
   - **Timeline:** 4 weeks from signature to data delivery
   - Create institution-specific success plan:
     - What outcomes we'll measure
     - When we'll deliver first report
     - How we'll validate accuracy together
     - Monthly review meeting schedule

2. **Data Collection & Validation** (Lead by: Product + Data team)
   - Start web scraping + public records lookup for each pilot's alumni base
   - Run data accuracy checks against institution's own records (where available)
   - Document any missing or inconsistent data
   - Create institution-specific data quality report
   - **Target:** 85%+ match rate with known alumni (validated against institution records)

3. **Market Research Continuation** (Lead by: Founder)
   - Document feedback from pilots #1-2:
     - What features are they using most?
     - What workflows are missing?
     - How is accuracy matching their expectations?
     - Are they seeing unexpected insights?
   - Interview 5-10 non-pilot prospects:
     - Why didn't they pilot with us?
     - What would change their mind?
     - What competitor are they considering?
     - Timeline to decision (if still engaged)

4. **Case Study Preparation** (Lead by: Marketer)
   - Begin documenting early wins from pilots #1-2:
     - Interesting alumni outcome trends discovered
     - How they're using data in planning
     - Feedback on ease of use
     - Plans post-pilot
   - Take screenshots/screens for case study

**Key Metrics (End of M4):**
- ✓ 2+ pilots live
- ✓ Data ingestion started (>300 alumni tracked)
- ✓ 85%+ data accuracy validation
- ✓ 1+ discovery calls from new prospects (word-of-mouth starting)
- ✓ Steering committee meeting 1 completed (customer satisfaction confirmed)

**CAC Budget:** $4,200 (1 customer × $4.2k CAC) [Total M1-4: $16,800]

## Go/No-Go Decision Framework

### Month 6 Checkpoint: Pilot Viability

**Proceed to Phase 2 (Scale to 10) if:**
- ✓ 5+ customers signed with average ACV ≥ $42,000
- ✓ CAC per customer ≤ $5,000
- ✓ Customer NPS ≥ 40
- ✓ Data accuracy ≥ 85%
- ✓ No critical product blockers identified

**Pause if:**
- ⛔ Only 2-3 customers signed (product/market fit risk)
- ⛔ Average ACV < $35,000 (pricing/positioning issue)
- ⛔ CAC > $6,000 (GTM inefficiency)
- ⛔ NPS < 30 (customer satisfaction risk)
- ⛔ Data accuracy < 75% (product risk)

### Month 12 Checkpoint: Commercial Viability

**Proceed to Year 2 Growth if:**
- ✓ 10+ customers acquired
- ✓ 80%+ of pilots renewing to commercial contracts
- ✓ Unit economics match assumptions (LTV:CAC >30:1)
- ✓ 5+ reference customers available
- ✓ Team capacity for 30-customer Year 2 target

**Reassess if:**
- ⛔ Only 6-7 customers acquired (slower than target)
- ⛔ <70% renewal rate (product/service issues)
- ⛔ ACV <$40,000 or CAC >$5,500 (economics deterioration)
- ⛔ Major product gaps identified in pilots

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pilot customers don't renew | Revenue cliff at M18 | Medium | Start renewal talks at M10; offer Year 2 discounts |
| Product technical issues | Customer churn; reputational damage | Low | Extensive QA; customer success onboarding; escalation support |
| Competitive entry | Price pressure; market saturation | Low | Speed to market; build network moat; acquire customers fast |
| Data accuracy below 85% | Customer dissatisfaction; refund pressure | Low | Rigorous QA process; transparent methodology; audit trail |
| CAC inflation above $5k | Economics deteriorate | Medium | Build referral program; improve conversion rate; warm outreach |
