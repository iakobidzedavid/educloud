## Campaign Execution Guidance

### Email Sending Schedule

**Day 1**: Send 6 research university emails (stagger by 30 min to avoid Gmail spam filter)  
**Day 2**: Send remaining 12 research university emails  
**Day 3**: Send 6 liberal arts emails  
**Day 4**: Send remaining 11 liberal arts emails  
**Day 5**: Send 6 community college emails  
**Day 6**: Send remaining 9 community college emails  

**Rationale**: Spacing prevents Gmail from flagging multiple sends as spam; allows monitoring for early bounces.

### A/B Testing Approach (Optional)

**Test Group A (25 prospects)**: Original subject line  
**Test Group B (25 prospects)**: Alternative subject line  

Track which subject line variant generates higher open rates by segment. Use winning variant for follow-up emails.

### Follow-Up Strategy

**Days 3-4**: For non-openers, no action (wait for additional opens)  
**Days 5-7**: For openers without clicks, send no follow-up (let natural interest develop)  
**Days 5-7**: For clickers without replies, send follow-up: "Quick follow-up on my email below—would love to connect briefly"  
**Days 7-10**: For replies without meetings scheduled, send: "Thanks for your interest. Are you available [times] next week for 15 min?"  
**Days 10-14**: For replies expressing interest but delaying, send: "Still keen to chat—here are some times that work for me [3 specific times]"

### Reply Handling Protocol

**Positive replies** ("Would love to chat," "Interested," "Can you tell me more"):
- Response time: Within 4 hours (same business day)
- Action: Propose 3 specific times, include Calendly link
- Tone: Professional, concise, respectful of their time

**Neutral replies** ("Interesting," "Tell me more," "Send me info"):
- Response time: Within 8 hours
- Action: Send 2-sentence explanation + Calendly link, ask for call
- Tone: Slightly more detailed, still brief

**Objection replies** ("Not a priority," "Already have a solution," "No budget"):
- Response time: Within 24 hours
- Action: Acknowledge concern, offer brief alternative angle, leave door open
- Tone: Respectful, not pushy, professional

**No reply to follow-ups** (after 2 follow-ups, 14+ days):
- Action: One final email: "Respect your busy schedule—feel free to reach out if priorities change"
- Then: Move to low-priority follow-up (quarterly check-in)

## Key Success Factors

1. **Personalization is critical**: Mention specific institution name, decision-maker name, segment-specific pain point
2. **No generic "Dear Prospect"**: Use actual names and titles
3. **Urgency framing**: "Next week" is better than "sometime in May"
4. **Clear CTA**: Include exact meeting link, not "let me know if interested"
5. **Respect their expertise**: Frame as conversation, not pitch
6. **Follow-up discipline**: Non-responders need 2 follow-ups over 10+ days
7. **Track everything**: Capture all data in tracking spreadsheet—this is your validation data
