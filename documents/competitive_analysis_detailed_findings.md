## Executive Summary

This competitive analysis examines **11 major platforms** operating in the alumni outcomes, higher education advancement, and workforce intelligence spaces. edu.cloud enters a fragmented market where:

1. **No single platform dominates alumni outcome measurement** — Current solutions focus on alumni engagement (Graduway, Salesforce Education Cloud) OR fundraising (Raiser's Edge) OR talent recruitment (Handshake, LinkedIn) — but NOT automated outcomes measurement at scale.

2. **Manual research and consulting dominate** — Institutions still rely heavily on surveys, manual tracking, and external consultants to measure alumni outcomes (the core problem edu.cloud solves).

3. **Significant cost and speed gaps exist** — Established platforms (Salesforce, Blackbaud) cost $50K-$100K+ annually and take months to implement. Manual consulting for outcome studies costs $25K-$100K+ per institution.

4. **Regulatory tailwinds present** — ACCJC, WASC, and other accreditors increasingly require alumni outcome data; FERPA compliance becomes a competitive advantage.

5. **Data enrichment is a hidden competitive frontier** — Most platforms rely on manual entry or one-way integrations; edu.cloud's approach (public records + web scraping + AI) is differentiated.

#### **1. Graduway (Gravyty)**

**What They Do:** Purpose-built alumni engagement platform with mentoring, community building, event management.

**Pricing:** $500-$5,000/month + $2K-$10K implementation  
**Customer Base:** 500+ institutions globally  
**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class user experience (4.4/5 ease of use)
- Specialized mentorship matching and program management

**Weaknesses:**

- Requires manual alumni data maintenance
- Does NOT track employment outcomes automatically
- Community-centric, not outcomes-centric

**Positioning:** "Build vibrant alumni communities" — engagement first, outcomes second

**Threat to edu.cloud:** LOW. Complementary (could integrate with edu.cloud for data enrichment). No automated outcome measurement.

**Pricing:** $50K-$100K+ annually (custom); Requires additional modules (Marketing Cloud, Tableau, MuleSoft, Data Cloud)  
**Customer Base:** 1,000+ higher ed customers (estimated)

**Positioning:** "Unified constituent management, AI-powered insights, Lifelong engagement through data-driven fundraising"

**Pricing:** Freemium (LinkedIn); LinkedIn Recruiter/Learning $300-$400+ per user/year; Custom enterprise pricing  
**Customer Base:** 800+ million LinkedIn members; 15,000+ enterprise clients

- Opt-in, not comprehensive

**Positioning:** "World's largest professional network, AI-powered talent intelligence, Lifelong learning at scale"

**Threat to edu.cloud:** MEDIUM. LinkedIn can show WHERE alumni are employed (if on platform). But edu.cloud is MORE comprehensive (public records + web + AI) and less dependent on alumni profile updates.

### Tier 6: Emerging Technologies

#### **10. Verifiable Credentials (Digital Diplomas)**

**What They Do:** Blockchain-based digital credentials, tamper-proof diplomas, portable educational records, instant employer verification.

**Pricing:** Emerging market; early pilots free; commercial platforms $0.50-$5 per credential issuance (estimated)  
**Customer Base:** Hundreds of early-adopter institutions globally (MIT, European universities)  
**Strengths:**
- Fraud-proof via cryptography
- Instant third-party verification (employers don't need to call university)
- Portable (not vendor lock-in)
- Privacy-preserving (W3C standards-based)
- Future-proof standard

**Weaknesses:**
- Still early-stage (adoption 2025+)
- Requires issuer adoption (universities must issue)
- Requires verifier adoption (employers must accept)
- Ecosystem fragmentation (W3C vs. EBSI vs. other standards)
- Only proves education, NOT employment outcome

**Positioning:** "Fraud prevention, Instant verification, Tamper-proof records, Portable credentials"

**Threat to edu.cloud:** LOW-MEDIUM. Complementary (verifies the degree, but not WHERE alumni went). Could integrate with edu.cloud to create "verified outcome portfolio."

#### **11. Connected (High School-to-Career Tracking)**

**What They Do:** Student success tracking across K-12 to college to career; labor market insights, earnings data, credential mapping.

**Pricing:** Proprietary subscription model; estimated partnership-based pricing  
**Customer Base:** 100+ school districts and colleges (estimated)  
**Strengths:**
- Labor market data integration (wages, occupational demand)
- IPEDS + state data partnerships
- Multi-institution pipeline visibility
- Policy-aligned (accreditor friendly)
- Predictive outcomes

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited to specific states/regions with data partnerships
- Labor market data may lag 1-2 years
- Privacy considerations for student data tracking
- Limited alumni engagement tools
- K-12 focus, not higher ed alumni focus

**Positioning:** "Labor market transparency, Credential value tracking, Student success prediction, Skills-to-jobs mapping"

**Threat to edu.cloud:** MEDIUM. Labor market data is valuable for measuring alumni outcomes. But Connected is K-12 focused and region-limited.
