## Hacker News Monitoring Workflow

### Weekly Monitoring Process

**Day 1 (Monday):**
1. Run Hacker News API query for Ask HN stories from past 7 days
2. Search for keywords: "higher education", "alumni", "university", "accreditation", "FERPA", "outcomes measurement"
3. Extract story IDs and titles
4. Flag 5-10 relevant discussions for deeper analysis

**Day 2 (Tuesday):**
1. Retrieve full story and comment text for flagged discussions
2. Read all comments (or top 20-30 highest-voted)
3. Extract:
   - Pain points mentioned (direct quotes)
   - Solutions referenced
   - Objections/concerns
   - Sentiment (positive/neutral/skeptical)
   - Decision-maker roles mentioned

**Day 3 (Wednesday):**
1. Document findings in tracking spreadsheet:
   - Discussion ID, title, date, score
   - Summary (2-3 sentences)
   - Pain points (with quotes)
   - Solutions mentioned
   - Sentiment breakdown
   - edu.cloud positioning opportunity

**Day 4 (Thursday):**
1. Aggregate weekly findings
2. Identify new pain points or shifts in sentiment
3. Update competitive intelligence file
4. Share key insights with marketing/sales teams

**Day 5 (Friday):**
1. Prepare weekly sentiment brief for leadership
2. Flag any urgent competitive threats or market shifts
3. Recommend messaging updates based on emerging themes

### A/B Test Design

**Test 1: Subject Line**
- Control: "[Institution Name] – Scale your alumni tracking from 500 to 5,000"
- Variant A: "How [University Type] moved from manual to automated alumni research"
- Variant B: "[Institution Name] – Track 100% of your alumni outcomes (not 10%)"
- Sample Size: 50 contacts per variant
- Run Time: 1 week
- Decision Criteria: Highest open rate wins

**Test 2: CTA Placement**
- Control: CTA in middle and end of email
- Variant A: CTA only at top of email
- Variant B: CTA prominent in middle only
- Sample Size: 50 contacts per variant
- Decision Criteria: Highest click-through and meeting booking rate

**Test 3: Social Proof**
- Control: Generic "most institutions see ROI in the first semester"
- Variant A: Specific peer institution example with metrics
- Variant B: Quote from actual customer (if available)
- Sample Size: 50 contacts per variant
- Decision Criteria: Highest reply rate and positive sentiment
