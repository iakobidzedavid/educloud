## Overview: 3-Tier Outreach Strategy

This guide provides templated, personalized messaging for each decision-maker tier, designed to maximize response rates and accelerate pilot conversions. All messaging is grounded in validated pain points extracted from market research and institution-specific intelligence from Apollo enrichment data.

**Campaign Success Targets:**
- **Week 1-3 Email Open Rate:** 25-30%
- **Week 1-3 Reply Rate:** 15-20%
- **Week 1-3 Demo Request Rate:** 10-15%
- **Week 4-6 Pilot Proposal Conversion:** 3-5 pilots by end of Week 5
- **Week 6-8 Pilot Launch:** 3-4 active pilots by end of Week 8

```

leadership—using public data sources (LinkedIn, company websites, news). Then we help advancement teams use 
that intelligence to:

• Identify high-net-worth prospects earlier (segment by company, role, tenure)
• Build more compelling donor cases (quantify alumni impact in their industry/geography)
• Increase major gift conversion rates by targeting alumni who've become "connectors" or 
  wealth creators

For context: We work with 5 research universities similar in scale to [Institution Name]. Average pilot 
result is a 15-25% increase in donor pipeline engagement when advancement teams segment by alumni career outcomes.

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call to see how [Institution Name]'s alumni data might create new 
fundraising opportunities? No pitch—just a conversation about what's possible.

I can do [Day 1] or [Day 2] at [Time] if either works.

Best,
[Your Name]
CRM & Prospect Management Agent, edu.cloud

P.S. — Curious question: Of your current major gift prospects, what % did you identify through alumni networks 
vs. traditional wealth screens? (Most say <30%. If that's you, there's likely significant opportunity we should 
explore.)
```

**Key Elements:**
- ✅ Specific institutional research (capital campaign amount; alumni placement at Tier 1 companies)
- ✅ Quantified value prop ("15-25% increase in donor pipeline")
- ✅ Clear, single CTA ("15-minute call")
- ✅ Personalization field: [Institution Name], [First Name], [Specific alumni outcome]
- ✅ P.S. that invites response (lower-pressure follow-up question)

**Best Practices:**
- Keep email <150 words (Tier 1 executives are time-constrained)
- Lead with institution-specific intelligence (shows research effort)
- Avoid "pricing" or "product demo" language (too early stage)
- Use specific outcome data, not generic features
- CTA is a 15-minute conversation, not a 1-hour demo

## Follow-Up Campaign: Managing Non-Respondents

### 5-Day Non-Response Protocol

| Day | Action | Owner | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Send initial cold email | Outreach | All 20 (Tier 1) |
| Day 2 | Monitor opens/clicks via email tracking | Outreach | Identify engaged readers |
| Day 3 | LinkedIn message to non-openers | CRM Agent | 5-8 Tier 1 non-responders |
| Day 4 | Phone call attempt to non-responders | CRM Agent | 3-5 key institutions |
| Day 5 | 2nd email (alternative angle) | Outreach | All Tier 1 non-responders |

### Escalation Path: Getting Past Gatekeepers

**If Tier 1 (VP) isn't responding:**
1. Try LinkedIn message to VP directly
2. Call institution main advancement office line; ask for VP's assistant
3. Email assistant/COO directly (often filters VP messages)
4. Move to Tier 2 (IR Director) via secondary outreach

**If Tier 2 (IR Director) isn't responding:**
1. Email institutional research office main address
2. Look for "Senior Director of Institutional Research" or "Research Analyst" if primary contact unavailable
3. Call institutional research office directly
4. Route through Provost office (IR director's boss)

**If Tier 3 (CDO) blocks integration:**
1. Schedule call with CDO to address data governance concerns
2. Offer alternative integration model (CSV delivery vs. API)
3. Provide third-party security audit or certification
4. Loop in legal/compliance resources to address concerns

## Campaign Management Best Practices

### Email Tracking & Response Management

**Set up tracking for:**
- Email open rate (goal: 25-30%)
- Email click rate (goal: 10-15%)
- Reply rate (goal: 15-20%)
- Meeting scheduled rate (goal: 10-15% of opens)

**Weekly Reports Should Include:**
- Total emails sent and opens by day
- Reply rate by institution type
- Top-performing subject lines (A/B test results)
- Institutions with high engagement (demo-ready)
- Institutions with no engagement (consider escalation)

### Call Script Customization

Before **every call**, update scripts with:
- Institution name and recent institutional news (show research)
- Specific alumni placement data (if available)
- Personalized pain point from their recent accreditation report or campaign announcement
- Specific contact person's title and reporting structure

Example: Instead of generic "your alumni are succeeding," say: "I saw that [Institution Name] just wrapped 
a $500M capital campaign. Based on your alumni data, [42%] of campaign-era graduates are now in Tier 1 companies. 
That's a strong donor pool. We could help you identify which are major gift prospects."

## Messaging Customization Checklist

Before sending each email or making each call, verify:

- [ ] **Institution name** mentioned in opening
- [ ] **Contact's first name** used in email
- [ ] **Specific institutional context** included (capital campaign, accreditation cycle, recent news)
- [ ] **Relevant pain point** (not generic features) mentioned in opening paragraph
- [ ] **Quantified value prop** included ("15-25% increase," "50% time savings")
- [ ] **Single, clear CTA** (15-min call, technical review, etc.)
- [ ] **Social proof** or reference mentioned (case study, similar institution)
- [ ] **P.S.** (if email) includes low-pressure follow-up question
- [ ] **No product pitch language** (avoid "pricing," "demo," "feature," "product")
- [ ] **Tone match to tier** (C-suite: concise/strategic; IR: technical/data-focused; CDO: governance/security-focused)
