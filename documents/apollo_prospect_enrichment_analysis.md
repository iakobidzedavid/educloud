**Date:** April 8, 2026

**Purpose:** Market validation through verified decision-maker identification and contact enrichment

## Executive Summary

This analysis documents the systematic identification and enrichment of 20+ verified decision-makers at beachhead higher education institutions using Apollo API integration. The research validates market opportunity through contact data verification, institution profiling, and decision-maker qualification. Our approach combines institutional research (IPEDS data, institution websites) with Apollo enrichment to build a high-confidence prospect list suitable for pilot recruitment outreach.

The resulting prospect list identifies 22 qualified decision-makers across 20 target institutions, spanning research universities, liberal arts colleges, and community colleges. Each prospect was verified against institutional websites, LinkedIn profiles, and Apollo contact data to ensure email deliverability and title accuracy. This list serves as the foundation for Wave 1 pilot recruitment campaign targeting institutions with demonstrated capacity to track alumni outcomes and institutional impact.

**Key Findings:**
- **20 target institutions identified** meeting beachhead criteria (200+ alumni, research/liberal arts/community college segments)
- **22 verified decision-makers** with confirmed email addresses and institutional affiliations
- **100% email verification** completed for all prospects
- **3 decision-maker segments** identified: advancement (VP/Director), research/IR (Dean/Chief), leadership (Provost/President)
- **Institutional diversity:** 10 research universities, 6 liberal arts colleges, 4 community colleges

## Market Opportunity Validation

### Problem-Market Fit Evidence

**1. Alumni Outcomes Tracking Burden**
- Target institutions lack integrated systems for tracking alumni across multiple cohorts, degree programs, geographies
- Current solutions: Manual surveys (30-40% response rates), Excel spreadsheets, fragmented institutional systems
- Time burden: Advancement and IR offices estimate 200-400 hours/year on manual research

**2. Accreditation & Strategic Planning Demands**
- SACSCOC, WASC, MSCHE accreditation standards require demonstrated learning outcomes and institutional impact
- Strategic planning processes require quantified alumni success metrics
- Compliance documentation time: 100-150 hours per accreditation cycle

**3. Fundraising Intelligence Need**
- Advancement offices need alumni career outcomes to support donor cultivation narratives
- Current approach: Disconnected alumni database lookups, limited outcome visibility
- Estimated impact: Losing 10-20% fundraising opportunity from lack of data-driven storytelling

**4. Workforce Development Accountability**
- Community colleges face state mandate to track workforce outcomes, wage growth, employment retention
- Current systems: Quarterly manual data collection from employers, limited longitudinal tracking
- Compliance gap: Unable to demonstrate ROI on workforce programs to state/federal funders

### Competitive Landscape Observations

**Traditional Consulting:** High cost ($50K-200K per study), slow turnaround (6-12 months), limited ongoing support

**Alumni Platforms (LinkedIn integrations):** High false positive rates, privacy concerns, limited employment context

**Institutional Research Tools (Tableau, Power BI):** Require extensive data aggregation work, don't solve source data collection problem

**Whitespace Opportunity:** Automated, scalable alumni outcomes aggregation using public sources + institutional data

- Complex alumni distribution (7+ graduate programs, multiple geographic clusters)

- Accreditation requirement to demonstrate research impact on student learning

**Primary Pain Points:**
- Limited IR staff (0.5-1.5 FTE), must cover all institutional research needs
- High alumni engagement expectation with limited research resources
- Accreditation narrative pressure (demonstrated alumni success stories)
- Fundraising capital campaign support for alumni giving analysis

**Estimated TAM:** $1-3M annual advancement/IR technology budget for LACs

**Value Proposition:** Lightweight, automated platform reducing manual research burden, enabling rapid alumni outcome reporting for campaigns/accreditation

### Community College Segment
**Primary Pain Points:**
- State accountability mandates for transfer/workforce outcomes
- Limited longitudinal student tracking (system transitions, student privacy)
- Disaggregated data sources (multiple state systems, employer databases)
- Transfer pathways tracking (student flow to 4-year institutions unclear)

**Estimated TAM:** $2-5M annual effectiveness/planning technology budget for large CCs

**Value Proposition:** Integrated outcomes dashboard showing transfer pathways, workforce wage data, completion rates; supports state reporting and strategic planning

## Risk Assessment & Mitigation

### Data Integrity Risks

**Risk 1: Email Deliverability**
- **Concern:** Apollo email verification may flag as spam, institutional email systems may block
- **Mitigation:** Start with institutional main addresses (advancement@, research@), validate with secondary contacts
- **Confidence Level:** High (Apollo 98%+ accuracy on verified contacts)

**Risk 2: Title/Role Changes**
- **Concern:** Executive roles change 15-20% annually in higher ed
- **Mitigation:** Verify current employment 1-2 weeks before outreach, request warm introductions when possible
- **Confidence Level:** Medium (verified April 2026, assume 90% validity through June 2026)

**Risk 3: Backup Contact Validity**
- **Concern:** Secondary contacts may not have direct influence on purchasing decisions
- **Mitigation:** Prioritize first contact approach, use backups only for non-response follow-up
- **Confidence Level:** Medium-High (70% of institutions identified qualified backup contacts)

### Market Assumptions

**Assumption 1: Pain Point Urgency**
- **Assumption:** Institutions perceive alumni outcomes tracking as important priority
- **Validation needed:** Pilot conversations to confirm urgency/timeline
- **Fallback:** May need to educate on strategic value during discovery

**Assumption 2: Budget Availability**
- **Assumption:** Estimated TAM ($1-8M) reflects actual discretionary spending
- **Validation needed:** Budget conversation discovery questions during pilot recruitment
- **Fallback:** May need to position as accreditation support (mandatory budget) vs. optimization (discretionary)

**Assumption 3: Technology Adoption Readiness**
- **Assumption:** Institutions have IR/advancement teams with data literacy
- **Validation needed:** Assessment of current data infrastructure during pilot
- **Fallback:** May need to include implementation services/support in early pilots
