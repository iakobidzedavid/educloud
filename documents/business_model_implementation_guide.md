# Business Model Implementation Guide
## From Analysis to Execution: Operationalizing LTV/CAC Targets
**edu.cloud Pilot Phase** | **April 2026**

---

## 1. Executive Overview

This guide translates the LTV/CAC financial analysis into specific, measurable actions for the sales, product, and customer success teams. It ensures the business model assumptions are tested, validated, and refined throughout the pilot phase.

**Goal:** Convert $78,750 average LTV and $2,200 blended CAC from theory to demonstrated reality by the end of the pilot.

### 2.2 Sales Playbook by Phase

**PHASE 1: MARKET VALIDATION (Months 1-2)**

**Target:** 3 institutions (1 research university, 1 liberal arts, 1 community college)

**Sales Process:**
```
Week 1: Prospect Identification
└─ Select 3 "champion" prospects with:
   • Clear advancement/research function
   • Leadership team that values data
   • Budget authority available
   • Likely case study potential

Week 2-3: Outreach & Initial Discovery
├─ Email outreach with value prop tied to their challenge
├─ Schedule 30-min discovery call
├─ Goals:
│  ├─ Understand their outcomes tracking challenges
│  ├─ Identify decision-makers
│  └─ Gauge budget / timeline
└─ Document: Prospect stage, decision criteria, timeline

Week 3-4: Product Demo & Deep Dive
├─ 60-min demo focused on their specific use case
├─ Show 2-3 relevant outcomes your system tracks
├─ Gather detailed requirements
├─ Goals:
│  ├─ Build internal champion
│  ├─ Identify customization needs
│  └─ Understand implementation scope
└─ Document: Technical requirements, stakeholder buy-in level

Week 4-6: Negotiation & Signature
├─ Send statement of work with:
│  ├─ Pricing (per pricing card)
│  ├─ Implementation timeline
│  ├─ Support terms
│  └─ Success metrics
├─ Navigate legal/compliance review (expect 2-3 week cycle)
├─ Goals:
│  ├─ Achieve signature
│  ├─ Document contract structure (upfront vs. quarterly)
│  └─ Schedule kick-off meeting
└─ Document: Actual ACV, contract terms, red flags

CAC Tracking (Phase 1):
└─ Target CAC: $4,000/institution
   ├─ High-touch discovery: $1,500 in staff time
   ├─ Legal customization: $1,500
   ├─ Demo/proof of concept: $500
   ├─ Admin/signature: $500
   └─ Expect to exceed budget by 25% due to customization

Success Metrics (Phase 1):
├─ 3 signed pilots by end of Month 2
├─ ACV: $22K average (range $16K-$28K acceptable)
├─ Sales cycle: <60 days preferred, <90 days acceptable
├─ Case study potential: 2/3 institutions
└─ Next phase readiness: Clear playbook documented
```

**PHASE 2: PLAYBOOK DEVELOPMENT (Months 3-4)**

**Target:** 4 additional institutions (apply learnings from Phase 1)

**Sales Process (Streamlined):**
```
Week 1-2: Prospect Batch Outreach
├─ Outreach to 12-15 prospects simultaneously
├─ Use templates developed from Phase 1 discovery conversations
├─ Goal: 6-8 initial conversations scheduled
└─ Track: Response rate, questions asked, objection patterns

Week 2-3: Discovery Call (30 min)
├─ Use standardized discovery script from Phase 1
├─ Ask 5-7 consistent questions across all prospects
├─ Qualify: Budget, timeline, need, decision-maker
└─ Track: Qualification scores, pain points mentioned

Week 3-4: Targeted Demo (45 min)
├─ Use templates/videos from Phase 1 success cases
├─ Focus demo on their specific use case (identified in discovery)
├─ Show similar institution outcomes (if available)
└─ Track: Feature requests, enthusiasm level, next steps clarity

Week 4-5: Streamlined Contracting
├─ Use standardized contract template from Phase 1
├─ Minimal customization (identify as project vs. standard feature)
├─ Faster legal review with established precedent
└─ Track: Legal review time, customization requests

CAC Tracking (Phase 2):
└─ Target CAC: $2,300/institution (-42% vs. Phase 1)
   ├─ Template-driven discovery: $800
   ├─ Standardized demo process: $400
   ├─ Streamlined legal: $700
   ├─ Admin/signature: $400
   └─ Overhead allocation: $0 (shift to fixed costs)

Success Metrics (Phase 2):
├─ 4 signed pilots by end of Month 4
├─ Average ACV: $21,500 (maintain pricing discipline)
├─ Sales cycle: 45-50 days average (improvement vs. Phase 1)
├─ Case study potential: 3/4 institutions
├─ Playbook maturity: 80% of steps documented and repeatable
└─ CAC reduction: Actual vs. Phase 1 documented
```

**PHASE 3: SCALE VALIDATION (Months 5-6)**

**Target:** 3+ additional institutions (prove repeatable model)

**Sales Process (Repeatable & Scalable):**
```
Week 1-2: Batch Outreach (Automated)
├─ Outreach to 20+ prospects with:
│  ├─ Personalized intro email (5 min per prospect)
│  ├─ Educational content about outcomes measurement
│  └─ Clear CTA to 30-min discovery call
├─ Goal: 8-10 discovery calls scheduled
└─ Track: Response rate, show rate, conversion rate

Week 2-3: Discovery Call (25 min)
├─ Use chatbot or structured form for initial qualification
├─ Live discovery only if qualified (budget + timeline + decision-maker)
├─ Use scoring system to identify A/B/C tier opportunities
└─ Track: Time per call, conversion rate to demo stage

Week 3-4: Templated Demo (40 min)
├─ Use recorded demo + live Q&A (vs. live-only in earlier phases)
├─ Demo library organized by institution type
├─ Auto-advance qualified prospects to contracting
└─ Track: Attendance, questions, conversion to proposal

Week 4-5: Self-Serve Contracting
├─ Pre-populated contract with institution details
├─ Self-serve signature platform (DocuSign, etc.)
├─ Automated legal review checklists
└─ Track: Signature time, legal questions

CAC Tracking (Phase 3):
└─ Target CAC: $1,500/institution (-65% vs. Phase 1)
   ├─ Automated outreach: $300

├─ Sales cycle: 35-40 days average (efficient)
├─ Case study potential: 2/3 institutions

```

```
│  ├─ Timeline & resource commitment (15 min)
│  └─ Next steps & open questions (10 min)
└─ Deliverable: Kick-off summary document with decisions recorded

Day 3-5: Data Mapping & Integration Planning (40 hours internal)
├─ Map their alumni database structure
├─ Identify data sources (CRM, HRIS, LinkedIn, public records)
├─ Document required field transformations
├─ Create technical integration roadmap
├─ Estimate custom development needs (yes/no decision)
└─ Deliverable: Technical specification document

Goal: Establish clear contract with institution; secure data access; identify any custom work
```

**Week 2: Data Integration & Initial Load**

```
Day 6-7: Data Extract & Transform (30 hours internal)
├─ Extract alumni data from their systems
├─ Cleanse and standardize to edu.cloud schema
├─ Validate record counts and data quality
├─ Run test load in staging environment
└─ Deliverable: Sample data report (100 records) for review

Day 8-9: Institutional Review & Approval (10 hours with customer)
├─ Customer reviews sample data for accuracy
├─ Verify outcome data sources (employment, education, location)
├─ Approve go-live of full dataset
└─ Deliverable: Institutional approval to load

Day 10: Production Load (5 hours internal)
├─ Load full alumni dataset to production
├─ Configure institutional settings & branding
├─ Set up user access roles
└─ Deliverable: System ready for exploration

Goal: Complete data integration with high quality; establish trust with institution
```

**Week 3: Training & Launch**

```
Day 11-12: Training Session (6 hours with customer, 4 hours prep)
├─ Live demo of system functionality for their alumni data
├─ Custom tutorials for their specific outcomes (career, education, giving, etc.)
├─ Hands-on exploration with their leadership team
├─ Q&A on capabilities and limitations
└─ Deliverable: Recorded training video for future reference

Day 13: Success Planning (4 hours with customer)
├─ Define ongoing use cases & reporting schedule
├─ Identify primary contact for support
├─ Set first quarterly business review (QBR) date
├─ Review expansion modules & timeline
└─ Deliverable: 12-month success plan document

Day 14: Go-Live
├─ Flipped switch to live access
├─ Monitoring for technical issues (first 48 hours)
├─ Welcome email with quick-start guide
└─ Deliverable: System live, customer trained, ongoing support begins

Goal: Deliver working system on time; build confidence for expansion; establish QBR rhythm
```

### 4.3 Variance Analysis

**If metrics are off-track, diagnose and adjust:**

```
SCENARIO: Pricing is 15% below target ($18.7K vs. $22K)

Root cause analysis:
├─ Competitive pressure? (Prospect shopping against alternatives)
├─ Messaging failure? (Value prop not landing with buyer)
├─ Buyer quality? (Targeting too small/budget-constrained institutions)
├─ Negotiation process? (Sales team accepting discounts too quickly)
└─ Market reality? (Real demand at $16-19K level)

Corrective actions:
├─ If competitive: Reinforce differentiation in ROI messaging
├─ If messaging: Refine discovery; understand more pain points
├─ If buyer quality: Tighten qualification criteria; focus on T1 institutions
├─ If negotiation: Add sales training on negotiation fundamentals
├─ If market reality: Adjust pricing model; rebrand at lower price point
└─ Timeline: Course-correct by Month 3

Impact on model:
├─ If ACV = $18.7K instead of $22K:
├─ LTV = $68,200 (vs. $81,180 base case)
├─ LTV:CAC = 31:1 (vs. 46.4:1) — still strong
├─ Year 1 revenue loss: ~$13K per customer
├─ For 10 customers: $130K annual revenue loss
└─ Decision: Acceptable variance or strategy adjustment?
```

## 6. Risk Mitigation & Contingency Plans

### 6.1 Key Risks & Mitigations

```
RISK: Pricing pressure (customers negotiate 20%+ below ACV)
├─ Probability: Medium (common in higher ed)
├─ Impact: Revenue -$44K on 10 customers (vs. $22K ACV)
├─ Mitigation 1: Intensive value-based selling in discovery
├─ Mitigation 2: Anchor pricing with ROI case studies
├─ Mitigation 3: Offer service-based discounting instead of price
├─ Decision gate: If >50% of customers below $20K ACV, pause recruiting
│                and adjust messaging
└─ Contingency: Shift to lower-price point positioning

RISK: Higher support costs (exceed 18% COGS target)
├─ Probability: Medium-High (customers always need more than expected)
├─ Impact: Gross margin drops from 82% to 75% (affects LTV)
├─ Mitigation 1: Self-service content library (reduce support requests)
├─ Mitigation 2: Structured SLA tiers (limit high-touch support)
├─ Mitigation 3: Success manager coverage model (1:10 ratio)
├─ Decision gate: If actual COGS >22% after Month 3, implement cost controls
└─ Contingency: Implement premium support tier to capture additional revenue

RISK: Longer sales cycles (>120 days vs. 50-day target)
├─ Probability: High (higher ed procurement is slow)
├─ Impact: Fewer contracts signed in 6-month pilot window
├─ Mitigation 1: Parallel track multiple prospects
├─ Mitigation 2: Simplify contract negotiation (use templates)
├─ Mitigation 3: Executive sponsor involvement in legal/compliance review
├─ Decision gate: If average cycle >100 days, escalate to executive
└─ Contingency: Extend pilot phase to 9 months; focus on 5-7 institutions

RISK: Higher churn than 15% annually (85% retention)
├─ Probability: Low-Medium (higher ed has stable budgets)
├─ Impact: LTV drops from $81,180 to $67,500 (>3 year drop)
├─ Mitigation 1: NPS tracking from Month 3 onward
├─ Mitigation 2: Quarterly business reviews to strengthen relationships
├─ Mitigation 3: Expansion module strategy to increase switching cost
├─ Decision gate: If churn indicated >20% in Year 1, investigate root cause
└─ Contingency: Implement customer success program; increase support

RISK: Competitive entry during pilot (new vendor emerges)
├─ Probability: Low (market still emerging)
├─ Impact: Pricing pressure; customer negotiations slower
├─ Mitigation 1: Move fast on first 5 customer signatures
├─ Mitigation 2: Build switching cost through custom integrations
├─ Mitigation 3: Case studies and proof points drive differentiation
├─ Decision gate: Monitor landscape; if competitor launches, accelerate marketing
└─ Contingency: Emphasize data quality and AI capabilities as moat
```
