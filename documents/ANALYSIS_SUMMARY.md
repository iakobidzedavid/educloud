## Implementation Timeline & Dependencies

### Phase 1: Infrastructure Deploy (Week 1-2)
**Owner:** Development team  
**Deliverable:** Testimonials component with placeholder quotes  
**Business Impact:** Prepares for pilot recruitment campaign; enables A/B testing

**Placeholder Strategy:**
- Use realistic scenarios from market research (NOT fabricated quotes)
- Label clearly: "Real Pilot Results Coming Soon"
- Emphasize "Coming Soon" messaging to drive urgency for pilot participation

### Phase 2: Outreach Campaign (Week 2-4)
**Owner:** Sales/outreach team  
**Deliverable:** Cold email campaign to 50 beachhead prospects  
**Business Impact:** First cohort of pilot customers; conversion measurement begins

**Testimonials Role:**
- Landing page displays placeholder testimonials
- A/B test impact on conversion rates
- Track pilot applications sourced from testimonials section

### Phase 3: Pilot Execution (Week 4-10)
**Owner:** Customer success team  
**Deliverable:** 12 pilot institutions complete 6-week program  
**Business Impact:** Real customer data collected; outcomes measured

**Testimonials Collection:**
- Weekly documentation of wins, metrics, customer quotes
- Structured outcome measurement (alumni tracked, time saved, business impact)
- Decision-point testimonials (why converting to contract)

### Phase 4: Testimonial Population (Week 10-12)
**Owner:** Marketing + customer success  
**Deliverable:** Real testimonials replace placeholders; case studies published  
**Business Impact:** Second outreach wave benefits from social proof; conversion improves

**Publishing Checklist:**
- [ ] Authenticity verified (institutional email, LinkedIn confirmation)
- [ ] Factual accuracy validated with customer
- [ ] Permission obtained + confidentiality respected
- [ ] Quality standards met (pain point + value + quantified outcome)

| Email conversion rate improvement | +20%+ | Week 6 (A/B test results) |

### Risk Factors & Mitigation

**Risk 1: Placeholder Testimonials Look Inauthentic**
- Mitigation: Use verified market research; clearly label "Coming Soon"
- Impact if occurs: Reduced landing page conversion; brand credibility damage
- Probability: Low (framework provided)

**Risk 2: Pilot Delays Reduce Testimonial Quality/Timing**
- Mitigation: Weekly documentation; partial quotes; weekly wins publishing
- Impact if occurs: Missed conversion window (Week 10-12); lost sales momentum
- Probability: Medium

**Risk 3: Institutions Reluctant to Provide Testimonials**
- Mitigation: Offer co-marketing; case study co-authoring; speaking opportunities
- Impact if occurs: Weak social proof; reduced conversion on second wave
- Probability: Low (institutions typically happy to share success)

**Risk 4: Testimonials Don't Drive Expected Conversion Improvement**
- Mitigation: A/B test landing page variants; identify bottleneck in funnel
- Impact if occurs: LTV analysis may not match projections; scale timeline delayed
- Probability: Medium (but worst-case CAC still acceptable at 1:14 ratio)

## Key Business Model Assumptions Under Validation

### Assumption 1: CAC Efficiency
- **Model:** CAC of $2,000 with 2-3 month payback
- **Testimonials role:** Reduce CAC through better landing page conversion
- **Validation method:** Track actual pilot acquisition costs; measure landing page A/B test
- **Success threshold:** CAC < $2,200; payback < 3 months

### Assumption 2: Pilot-to-Contract Conversion
- **Model:** 70% of pilots convert to annual contracts
- **Testimonials role:** Provide proof-of-concept; reduce implementation risk
- **Validation method:** End-of-pilot decision tracking; contract signatures
- **Success threshold:** 70%+ conversion rate; average ACV $18K+

### Assumption 3: Customer Lifetime Value
- **Model:** 3-year LTV of $40K+ per customer
- **Testimonials role:** Validate retention through documented satisfaction
- **Validation method:** Renewal tracking; expansion inquiries; NPS measurement
- **Success threshold:** 90%+ renewal rate; 20%+ expansion

### Assumption 4: Competitive Positioning
- **Model:** edu.cloud wins 70%+ competitive evaluations
- **Testimonials role:** Explicit differentiation vs. alternatives
- **Validation method:** Win/loss interviews; competitive comparison testimonials
- **Success threshold:** 70%+ choose edu.cloud; cite speed/automation/cost
