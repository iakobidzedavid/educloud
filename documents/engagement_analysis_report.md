## Part 3: Prospect Segmentation Strategy for edu.cloud

### Institution Type Definitions (IPEDS Classification)

#### Segment 1: Research Universities (Target: 15-20 of 50 contacts)
- Carnegie Classification: R1, R2, D/PU (Doctoral/Professional Universities)
- Typical enrollment: 10,000-50,000+ students
- Alumni network size: 100,000+ living alumni
- Budget authority: Provost, VP Advancement, VP Institutional Research
- Decision timeline: 8-12 weeks
- Pilot likelihood: High (strong compliance/rankings motivation)

**WHY THIS SEGMENT MATTERS**:
- Largest alumni bases = greatest outcome tracking need
- Accreditation/rankings drive urgent data needs
- Higher budget for institutional services ($50K-500K+ pilot budgets typical)
- If successful, become reference customers for other R1s

**Messaging Focus**:
- "Track 100K+ alumni career outcomes automatically"
- "Generate accreditation-ready impact reports in days"
- "Benchmark your institutional outcomes against peer group"

#### Segment 2: Liberal Arts Colleges (Target: 15-20 of 50 contacts)
- Total enrollment: 1,000-5,000 students
- Alumni network size: 10,000-60,000 alumni
- Budget authority: VP Development/Advancement, VP Enrollment
- Decision timeline: 6-10 weeks
- Pilot likelihood: High (strong alumni engagement motivation)

**WHY THIS SEGMENT MATTERS**:
- Alumni engagement critical for fundraising (40-70% of donations from alumni)
- Smaller organizations = faster decision-making
- Hungry for competitive differentiation in rankings
- Strong fit for early pilots (smaller implementation scope)

**Messaging Focus**:
- "Know where your alumni are winning careers"
- "Personalize alumni engagement with real outcome data"
- "Demonstrate institutional value to donors"

#### Segment 3: Community Colleges (Target: 10-15 of 50 contacts)
- Total enrollment: 3,000-15,000 students
- Alumni network size: 5,000-50,000+ (often attendance-weighted)
- Budget authority: VP Institutional Effectiveness, Board of Trustees liaison
- Decision timeline: 4-8 weeks (faster, more bureaucratic)
- Pilot likelihood: Moderate (cost-conscious, compliance-driven)

**WHY THIS SEGMENT MATTERS**:
- Compliance requirements as strict as universities (SACSCOC, accreditation)
- Often underserved by existing solutions (expensive platforms)
- Workforce development outcomes tracking critical
- Good early-adopter segment (smaller budgets, faster decisions)

**Messaging Focus**:
- "Prove workforce outcomes efficiently & affordably"
- "Comply with workforce development reporting requirements"
- "Understand career pathways for continuous improvement"

## Part 4: Campaign Tracking Template & Implementation Guide

### A. Required Data Capture Infrastructure

To execute proper engagement analysis going forward, implement this tracking system:

#### Email Campaign Tracking Spreadsheet Structure

**File**: `outreach_campaign_tracking_[SEGMENT]_[DATE].csv`

```
Prospect Name,Title,Institution,Institution Type,Email,Decision-Maker (Y/N),
Send Date,Send Time,Template Used,Email Open,Open Date,Open Time,
Click Tracking Link,Click Date,Reply Received,Reply Date,Reply Content Summary,
Meeting Scheduled,Meeting Date,Meeting Duration,Meeting Notes,
Engagement Score,Follow-up Status,Pilot Interest Level,Source of Contact
```

**Template Fields Explained:**

| Field | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Prospect Name | Who was contacted | Dr. Jennifer Martinez |
| Title | Job title | VP of Institutional Research |
| Institution | College/university name | University of Minnesota |
| Institution Type | R1/Liberal Arts/Community | R1 Research University |
| Email | Contact address | j.martinez@umn.edu |
| Decision-Maker (Y/N) | Can approve pilot? | Y |
| Send Date | Campaign send date | 2026-04-15 |
| Email Open | Did they open email? | Y |
| Click Tracking | Clicked CTA link? | Y |
| Reply Received | Did they respond? | Y |
| Meeting Scheduled | Is call/meeting booked? | Y |
| Engagement Score | Overall interest signal (1-5) | 4 |
| Pilot Interest Level | Low/Medium/High/Strong | Medium |

#### Key Metrics to Calculate

**Open Rate** = (Emails Opened / Emails Sent) × 100
- Example: 8 opens / 18 research university prospects = 44%

**Click-Through Rate (CTR)** = (Clicks / Emails Opened) × 100
- Example: 3 clicks / 8 opens = 37.5%

**Reply Rate** = (Replies Received / Emails Sent) × 100
- Example: 2 replies / 50 total = 4%

**Engagement Score per Prospect** (1-5 scale):
- **1 = No engagement**: No open, no click, no reply
- **2 = Minimal**: Open only
- **3 = Interested**: Open + click
- **4 = Very interested**: Reply without meeting scheduled
- **5 = Highly engaged**: Reply + meeting scheduled

**Segment Comparison Matrix**:
```
Segment              | Opens | CTR | Replies | Meetings | Avg Score | Pilot Ready
Research University  | 44%   | 38% | 11%     | 6%       | 3.8       | 1-2
Liberal Arts        | 35%   | 29% | 6%      | 3%       | 3.2       | 0-1
Community College   | 28%   | 22% | 4%      | 2%       | 2.6       | 0
```

- Weekly export of tracking data to master spreadsheet

- Identify high-engagement prospects for follow-up prioritization

#### Step 4: Analysis & Refinement
- Monthly segment performance review
