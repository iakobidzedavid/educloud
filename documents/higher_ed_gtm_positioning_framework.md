## Part 1: Positioning Framework

### Primary Positioning Statement

**"The Only Alumni Outcomes Platform Built for Privacy and Compliance"**

**Expanded positioning:**

edu.cloud is the first alumni outcomes platform purpose-built for higher education compliance, privacy, and institutional control. We automatically track and aggregate alumni career outcomes across public records, institutional systems, and optional alumni self-reporting—while maintaining FERPA compliance, alumni consent, and data security from day one.

Unlike consulting firms (slow, expensive) and generic CRM platforms (not purpose-built), edu.cloud delivers:
- **Continuous measurement** instead of annual reports
- **Real-time insights** instead of 6-month-old data
- **Compliance by design** instead of risk and liability
- **Dramatic ROI** in first 12 months

### Why This Positioning Works

#### Against Consulting Firms
- **Price:** We cost 5-10x less (software vs. people)
- **Speed:** Weeks, not months
- **Frequency:** Continuous, not campaign-based
- **Control:** Institutions own their data
- **Sustainability:** Repeatable, measurable process

#### Against Generic CRMs (Salesforce, Ellucian)
- **Purpose-built:** Designed specifically for alumni outcomes, not generic customers
- **Out-of-box ready:** Works without 6-month consulting implementation
- **Compliance focus:** FERPA and privacy built in, not bolted on
- **Cost of ownership:** Lower ($15K-30K/year vs. $50K+/year)

#### Against Fragmentation/DIY
- **Public records integration:** We handle the legal/technical complexity
- **Privacy management:** Alumni consent built-in
- **Quality assurance:** Validation and accuracy checks
- **Institutional support:** Dedicated higher ed team

## Part 4: Competitive Battlecards

### Battlecard 1: Consulting Firms (Gallup, Hanover, Intrado, etc.)

**Their positioning:** "Expert research, comprehensive analysis, custom recommendations"

**Our counter:**
| Their advantage | How we counter |
|---|---|
| Brand / prestige | We deliver better data at 1/5 the cost |
| Custom analysis | We give you the data; you (or your team) do the analysis. It's yours. |
| External credibility | Our compliance audit is from a third party, just like their report |
| Handholding | Our product is simple enough you don't need handholding |

**How to win:**
- "You're paying for a report. We're giving you a system."
- "Reports are static; our data is continuous."
- "You maintain control and can iterate analysis yourself."
- Price: "You'll spend $40K-60K with a consultant for one report. We cost $15-30K/year for continuous measurement."

**When to walk away:** If they're locked into a multi-year consulting contract, it's hard to win until renewal.

---

### Battlecard 2: Salesforce (or similar generic CRM)

**Their positioning:** "One platform for all customer relationships (including alumni)"

**Our counter:**
| Their advantage | How we counter |
|---|---|
| Integrated ecosystem | We're integrated INTO Salesforce; we add outcomes capabilities Salesforce doesn't have |
| Broad functionality | We're focused on alumni outcomes. That focus = better product |
| Implementation support | Our implementation is 30 days, not 6+ months |
| Negotiating power | We're best-of-breed for alumni outcomes; they can't negotiate that away |

**How to win:**
- "Salesforce is your CRM. We're your alumni outcomes platform. You need both."
- "Salesforce is complex and expensive to customize for alumni. We do this out of the box."
- "Your Salesforce team won't prioritize alumni outcomes. We will."
- "We integrate with Salesforce and 5+ other systems. You're not choosing between platforms."

**When to walk away:** If they've invested heavily in Salesforce alumni customization and you can't demonstrate clear ROI difference.

---

### Battlecard 3: DIY / Open Source / LinkedIn Automation

**Their positioning:** "We built something internal / We use LinkedIn / We built custom scripts"

**Our counter:**
| Their advantage | How we counter |
|---|---|
| Low cost | You're not counting the hidden cost of staff time and maintenance |
| Customization | We're more flexible than you think; and our out-of-box features are valuable |
| No vendor risk | Our platform risk is lower than your internal system becoming unmaintained |
| Data you own | You own data with us too; we're just the aggregation layer |

**How to win:**
- "Your LinkedIn automation gets 15% response rate and takes 5 hours/week. We give you 60-70% coverage from public records alone, automatically."
- "Your custom script works until someone leaves. Then it breaks. Our system has support, maintenance, compliance updates."
- "You're paying salary for someone to maintain this. What if that person got sick?"
- "FERPA compliance is complex. Are you confident your approach is defensible?"

**When to walk away:** If they've built a highly customized system that genuinely works and they're very invested in maintaining it, it's hard to win on pure capability.

## Part 5: Discovery Questions (Sales Qualification)

### Must-Ask Questions

1. **"How do you currently track alumni outcomes?"**
   - *Why:* Understand current state, identify pain
   - *Listen for:* Manual processes, multiple systems, time/cost complaints

2. **"What does accreditation require from you, and how do you currently meet that requirement?"**
   - *Why:* Understand regulatory driver and pain point
   - *Listen for:* Time-intensive, consultant help needed, stress about compliance

3. **"If you could measure alumni success instantly and accurately, what would you do differently?"**
   - *Why:* Understand vision and ROI potential
   - *Listen for:* Fundraising focus, strategic insight focus, or compliance focus (tells you how to position)

4. **"What's your biggest concern about outsourcing alumni data management?"**
   - *Why:* Understand key objections (usually compliance or control)
   - *Listen for:* FERPA, security, data privacy—prep your compliance story

5. **"What does your ideal alumni platform need to do to replace your current process?"**
   - *Why:* Understand must-have vs. nice-to-have
   - *Listen for:* Integration, automation, accreditation-readiness priorities

### Qualification Criteria

**Good fit (high probability of deal):**
- ✅ Currently using multiple systems for alumni tracking
- ✅ Spending >$30K/year on current alumni management
- ✅ Have accreditation reporting deadline within 12 months
- ✅ Clear champion (advancement, IR, or compliance leader)
- ✅ Budget allocated or willingness to find budget

**Poor fit (low probability):**
- ❌ Completely satisfied with current process
- ❌ No compliance pressure (unlikely for accredited institutions, but possible)
- ❌ Zero budget allocated
- ❌ No clear sponsor/champion
- ❌ Legacy system integration requirements we can't meet

## Part 6: Objection Handling Scripts

### Objection 1: "We're not ready for a new system yet"

**Root cause analysis:** Usually means lack of urgency or unclear ROI

**Response:**
"I understand. Most institutions aren't 'ready' until they hit an accreditation cycle or a major compliance event. Here's what I've seen work: Could we run a quick 30-day pilot where we show you what your alumni outcomes data could look like—with no implementation commitment? If it's not valuable, we stop. If it is, you've got the data to make a real decision. Fair?"

---

### Objection 2: "This is interesting, but we need to talk to [committee/other department]"

**Root cause:** Multi-stakeholder buy-in needed (common in higher ed)

**Response:**
"That makes total sense. Higher ed decisions involve multiple stakeholders. Let's get [IR director], [compliance officer], and [advancement director] on a call together. We can show how this helps each of them specifically—accreditation prep, compliance certainty, and fundraising insight. Does [date] work for a quick 30-min walkthrough?"

---

### Objection 3: "How is this FERPA-compliant? Alumni data is sensitive."

**Root cause:** Real concern, not objection

**Response:**
"Great question. FERPA actually distinguishes between student records (protected) and alumni records (more flexible once graduated). What makes us different: we built FERPA compliance into product design from day one. We can show you:
- Our FERPA compliance documentation (written with higher ed counsel)
- How our consent management works
- Our third-party security audit
- Why our data sources (public records) are FERPA-safe
Would it help to schedule a call with our compliance officer and your legal team? That's pretty standard for us."

---

### Objection 4: "What if we integrate this and you go out of business?"

**Root cause:** Vendor risk concern

**Response:**
"Real concern. Here's how we manage risk:
- We're VC-backed [if applicable] with [X years] runway
- Your data never leaves your control; you can export anytime
- We publish our data schema publicly
- Our integrations are standards-based (REST API, OAuth)
- If anything happened to us, your data isn't locked in
We've never had a customer stuck in a vendor situation; it's not how we think about product."

---

### Objection 5: "How much does this cost?"

**Root cause:** Budget question (good sign)

**Response:**
"Great question. Our pricing is based on institution size and alumni base:
- Small institution (under 1K alumni): ~$15K/year
- Mid-size (1K-5K alumni): ~$25K/year
- Large (5K+ alumni): ~$35-50K/year
That's all-in—no implementation fees, no per-record charges.
Most institutions break even in 6-12 months by eliminating manual work.
Where do you fall in that range?"

## Part 8: Pricing Strategy (From Market Sentiment)

### Pricing Principles

**Based on HN community feedback:**
1. **Value-based, not consumption-based** — Institutions want predictability
2. **Undercut consulting cost** — Should feel like 5-10x better value
3. **Transparent** — No hidden per-record or per-query charges
4. **Scale with alumni size** — Bigger institutions, bigger investment

### Proposed Pricing Model

**Tier 1: Starter (Under 2,500 alumni)**
- Price: $15,000/year
- Included: Data aggregation, basic dashboards, 2 users
- Support: Email + community

**Tier 2: Growth (2,500-10,000 alumni)**
- Price: $30,000/year
- Included: Everything in Starter + advanced analytics, 5 users, custom integrations
- Support: Priority email + quarterly check-ins

**Tier 3: Enterprise (10,000+ alumni)**
- Price: $50,000-75,000/year
- Included: Everything in Growth + dedicated success manager, custom reporting, API access
- Support: Phone + dedicated engineer

**Rationale:**
- Tier 1 breaks even vs. 0.5 FTE of manual work in 6 months
- Tier 2 covers most comprehensive universities and smaller research institutions
- Tier 3 handles large state systems and R1 universities
- All tiers undercut consulting cost (which is $40K+ per year alone)

## Part 9: Sales Process & Timeline

### Typical Sales Cycle (Higher Ed = 4-6 months)

**Week 1-2: Discovery**
- Initial conversation, understand current state
- Identify pain points (accreditation, data fragmentation, ROI)
- Determine stakeholders (IR, compliance, advancement)
- Gauge budget availability

**Week 3-4: Deeper Qualifying**
- Multi-stakeholder call (IR, compliance, advancement leaders)
- Demo showing their specific use case
- Technical requirements discussion with IT

**Week 5-8: Pilot Agreement**
- Propose 30-day pilot
- Low-commitment contract ($5K-10K pilot fee)
- Technical integration planning

**Week 9-12: Pilot Execution**
- Data integration (usually complete within 2 weeks)
- First insights delivered
- Internal edu.cloud + prospect collaboration

**Week 13-16: Decision & Close**
- Pilot review meeting with all stakeholders
- ROI discussion
- Contract negotiation
- Full implementation planning

### Accelerators (How to Shorten Cycle)

- **Accreditation deadline pressure** → Cuts cycle by 50% if accreditation is within 6 months
- **Budget year-end spending** → Cuts cycle if budget must be spent by June 30 (fiscal year-end)
- **New compliance mandate** → Cuts cycle if regulatory deadline is imminent
- **Executive champion** → Having a "sponsor" who wants this reduces committee delay
